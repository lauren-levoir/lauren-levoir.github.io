About Me

Hi, my name is Lauren LeVoir, and I am a senior at the University of Wisconsin–Madison double majoring in Computer Science and Information Science with a certificate in Digital Studies.

I’m passionate about ethical computing and two summers ago conducted research at Visual Information Processing (VIP) Labs on the University of Minnesota, where I explored new ways to caption images using sequential prompting to reduce bias in Computer Vision models. Beyond the classroom, I serve on the executive board for Women in Business–Technology, where I’ve helped grow our membership from 30 to 70 and created opportunities for women to connect, learn, and lead in tech. Last summer, I gained industry experience as a software engineering intern at Medtronic, where I sharpened my technical and problem-solving skills in a professional setting.

Looking ahead, I am excited to continue building inclusive, human-centered technologies and to collaborate with diverse teams to make a positive impact through AI and innovation.

Project Showcase

VISOGENDER 2.0

As a researcher in Visual Image Processing Labs at the University of Minnesota, I enhanced multi-person image captioning models with a focus on accessibility and fairness using sequential prompting. I identified limitations in the original VISOGENDER. paper, where individuals in stereotypical roles: like doctors, were often misclassified as male. By developing VISOGENDER 2.0, I improved the model’s accuracy in these cases, ensuring captions more accurately reflected each person’s identity. These improvements make AI-generated image descriptions more accessible for blind users, providing inclusive and representative information for all audiences.
